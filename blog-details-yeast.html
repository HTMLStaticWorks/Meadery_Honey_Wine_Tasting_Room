The Yeast Nutrition Guide

Published on March 12, 2026 by Arthur Sterling

Unlike grape juice, which is naturally rich in nutrients, honey is essentially pure sugar. While this makes it a stable food source for bees, it presents a challenge for yeast. To produce clean, aromatic mead, we must act as 'yeast dietitians.'

The Nitrogen Deficit

Yeast requires nitrogen to build proteins and reproduce. Without it, the yeast becomes stressed and begins to produce hydrogen sulfide—resulting in a 'rotten egg' smell that can ruin a batch. We use YAN (Yeast Assimilable Nitrogen) testing to determine exactly how much supplemental nutrition our must needs.

"Healthy yeast makes happy mead."

Organic vs. Inorganic Nutrition

At Nectar, we prefer organic nutrition sources like hulls from dead yeast cells. These provide a slow-release form of nitrogen that leads to a steady, controlled fermentation. Inorganic sources can cause a 'sugar rush' effect, leading to excessive heat and the production of harsh alcohols.

By carefully managing the temperature and nutrient additions over the first 72 hours of fermentation, we ensure our yeast remains healthy and productive, resulting in a mead that is smooth, refined, and free of off-flavors.
